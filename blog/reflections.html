2. What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

In a working environment, technical skills are just one part of the job. Being able to communicate effectively and being someone others enjoy working with are just as important — often key factors when getting hired.

3. What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I wouldn’t say there were major surprises for me.

4. What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Finding the time and energy to complete it.

5. Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I completely understand why it’s included in the curriculum, especially for a younger crowd who may not have had much professional experience yet. At the end of the day, when hiring someone, you're choosing a person you'll spend around eight hours a day with. Hiring managers often prefer someone who is great to work with and communicates well, even if they’re a little less technically advanced. Technical skills can usually be taught more easily than interpersonal skills. For a school aiming to get people hired, focusing on these concepts is valuable.

6. Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

For me personally, it did feel a little bit like a waste of time. I already work in a people-focused role where I spend most of my week developing human skills — managing sensitive issues with clients, coordinating between teams, and communicating effectively. Because of this, when it comes to course-related work, I’m very eager to dive deeper into technical skills rather than revisiting concepts I’m already applying daily, specially considering I have limited time to spend on course material in general.
